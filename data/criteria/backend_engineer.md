# Backend Engineer: hiring criteria

## Core skills
Strong programming ability in a backend language (Python, Go, Java, or similar).
Solid understanding of HTTP APIs and how to design them: resource modeling,
versioning, pagination, error handling, idempotency. Comfortable with relational
databases (schema design, indexing, and reasoning about query performance), and
aware of when a different store fits better.

## Systems and operations
Experience running services in production, not just building them: logging, metrics,
tracing, and being on call for something real. Understands failure modes of
distributed systems (retries, timeouts, backpressure, idempotency) at least well
enough to not make the obvious mistakes. Familiar with containers and a CI/CD flow.

## Engineering practice
Writes tests as a matter of course and treats them as part of the work, not an
afterthought. Reviews code thoughtfully. Can break a large problem into shippable
pieces and actually ships them. Writes clearly: design docs, PR descriptions, and
incident notes are part of the job.

## Seniority signals
Junior: can deliver well-scoped tasks with guidance.
Mid: owns features end to end and needs little hand-holding.
Senior and above: sets technical direction, mentors, and is trusted with ambiguous,
high-impact problems. Look for evidence of ownership and judgment, not just years.

## What we don't require
A specific degree. Strong hands-on experience outweighs credentials for this role.
Familiarity with our exact stack. Good engineers pick up a new framework quickly.
