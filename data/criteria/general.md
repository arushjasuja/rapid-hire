# General hiring bar

Across every role we screen for a few things that aren't role-specific.

**Ownership.** Evidence that the person takes something ambiguous and drives it to
done, rather than waiting to be told each step. Shipping real software that real people
used is the strongest signal.

**Communication.** Can explain a technical decision in writing, clearly, to someone who
wasn't in their head. Most of the job is communication; resumes and portfolios that
show clear writing count for a lot.

**Collaboration.** Works well with others, gives and takes code review in good faith,
and doesn't need to be the smartest person in every room.

**Growth.** A trajectory that shows learning over time, whether increasing scope,
responsibility, or depth, matters more than any single credential. A degree is a plus,
never a requirement.

**Honesty about limitations.** We'd rather see a candidate who's clear about what they
haven't done than one who oversells. Grounded self-assessment is a green flag.
